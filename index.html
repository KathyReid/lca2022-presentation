Creating Communities

Image: Left: On Purposeful Systems, Middle: Russell Ackhoff, Right: Fred Emery

    Communities have goals ...

    ... and choices about how to reach them

  1. Define the community's goals and purposes.
  2. And the values and choices it will and won't use to attain those goals and purposes.

Stocks
and
Flows

Attribution: Upper Kalimna Falls, Otway Ranges, 2012 by Kathy Reid

  1. Stocks and flows apply to many community resources.
  2. Understand your stocks and flows, and where you might want to redirect them.

Cardinality and connection

Attribution:
String Music
via Elvis Kennedy on Flickr CC-BY-NC

  1. Cardinality shows how closely your community is interconnected.
  2. Understand where people may not be connected to the community, and find ways to strengthen those connections.

Requisite variety

Attribution:
Sparkle
via Peter Burka on Flickr CC-BY-SA

  1. Requisite variety helps systems be resilient to shocks - unexpected, destabilising events.
  2. The greater the variety in your community, the more resilient your community will be.

Concluding Communities

Entropy

Attribution:
Entropy
via Bill Swingle on Flickr CC-BY-NC-ND

  1. Be aware of the force of entropy in your community, and what the balance of opposing forces is.
  2. Choosing when to end your community allows the process to be managed carefully, gently and thoughtfully.
